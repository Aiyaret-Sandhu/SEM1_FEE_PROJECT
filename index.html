Proper vegetable storage is a crucial aspect of maintaining their freshness and nutritional value. Creating an optimal environment involves a delicate balance of factors. Vegetables benefit from cool, dry places, such as a refrigerator's crisper drawer or a well-ventilated pantry. It's essential to separate ethylene-producing fruits from ethylene-sensitive vegetables to prevent premature ripening. While ensuring good airflow with perforated bags or containers, some veggies like carrots and celery thrive with a bit of moisture, but excessive dampness can lead to spoilage...
